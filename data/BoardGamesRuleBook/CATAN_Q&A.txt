Board_game: CATAN
Q: Can you play a development card the same turn you buy it?
A: No, you must wait until your next turn (except Victory Points).

Q: Can you use Year of Plenty to take two of the same resource?
A: Yes, you may choose any two, even duplicates.

Q: What does the Monopoly card do?
A: It lets you take all cards of one resource type from every player.

Q: Can you build multiple roads in one turn?
A: Yes, as long as you have enough resources.

Q: Can you steal from a player with no resource cards?
A: Yes, but you get nothing.

Q: Can you win by revealing multiple Victory Point cards mid-turn?
A: Yes, you can reveal them anytime to reach 10 points and win.

Q: Can you trade after building?
A: Yes, you can trade and build in any order.

Q: Can you downgrade a city back to a settlement?
A: No, upgrades are permanent.

Q: Can you use a harbor without building a settlement there?
A: No, you must control the harbor with a settlement or city.

Q: Can you build a road through another player’s settlement?
A: No, you cannot pass through another player’s settlement or city.

Q: Can you move the robber onto a hex where you have a settlement?
A: Yes, but it still blocks that hex from producing.

Q: Can you trade with another player during their turn?
A: Yes, trading can happen on any player’s turn, but only the current player can trade with the bank.

Q: Can you play multiple development cards on the same turn?
A: No, only one development card per turn (except Victory Points).

Q: Can you build a settlement and upgrade it to a city in the same turn?
A: Yes, if you have enough resources for both actions.

Q: Can you use a Knight card before rolling the dice?
A: No, you must roll first, then play the Knight.

Q: What happens if two players tie for Longest Road?
A: Neither gets the card until one has the clear lead.

Q: How are roads placed?
A: Each road must connect to one of your existing roads, settlements, or cities.

Q: Can a player build disconnected roads?
A: No, every new road must connect to your existing network.

Q: What does a Year of Plenty card do?
A: It gives you any two resource cards from the bank.

Q: What is needed to upgrade a settlement to a city?
A: 3 ore and 2 grain.

Q: Can you build during another player’s turn?
A: No, only on your own turn.

Q: What happens if the bank runs out of development cards?
A: No more can be purchased until someone uses or returns one (rare).

Q: What happens if a 7 is rolled and you have 8 cards?
A: You must discard 4 cards (half, rounded down).

Q: Can you lose the Longest Road card?
A: Yes, if another player builds a longer continuous road.

Q: Can you trade with the bank during another player’s turn?
A: No, only the active player can trade with the bank.

Q: Can the robber block your own settlement?
A: Yes, you also get no production from that hex.

Q: What happens if the resource deck runs out?
A: Players cannot collect that resource until others return some to the bank.

Q: Can you build roads in any direction?
A: Yes, as long as each connects to your existing network.

Q: How many cities can you have at most?
A: 4, since you only have 4 city pieces.

Q: What happens if the robber is placed on the desert?
A: The desert produces nothing, and it remains blocked.

Q: Can you trade after building on your turn?
A: Yes, trading and building can occur in any order.

Q: Can you use a Knight card to move the robber onto the same hex?
A: No, you must move it to a different hex.

Q: What happens if two players both reach 10 points in the same round?
A: The player whose turn it is wins immediately.

Q: Can the robber be placed on a hex with no adjacent settlements?
A: Yes, but it only blocks production — no stealing occurs.

Q: What happens if someone cuts your Longest Road in half?
A: You lose the title until you rebuild and surpass them.

Q: Can you play a Monopoly card and then trade in the same turn?
A: Yes, that’s allowed after the effect resolves.

Q: Can you use Road Building to block another player?
A: Yes, as long as your roads follow placement rules.

Q: What happens if the bank runs out of a resource type?
A: That resource cannot be given out until someone pays it back.

Q: Can you play development cards after you roll a 7?
A: Yes, after resolving the robber phase.
